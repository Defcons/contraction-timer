Technique

Find the right muscles

  • Imagine gently stopping wind and urine at the same time — a "squeeze and lift" inside, up and forward.
  • Nothing should visibly move: no clenching buttocks, thighs or stomach.
  • Only use stopping urine mid-flow to identify the muscles once — never as the exercise itself.

While exercising

  • Breathe normally the whole time — counting out loud helps.
  • Relaxing fully between holds matters as much as the squeeze.
  • Start lying down; progress to sitting, then standing as it gets easier.

After birth

  • Vaginal birth: start as soon as it feels comfortable — even within days. Never with a catheter in, and stop if it hurts.
  • C-section: wait for your check-up clearance (usually ~6 weeks).
  • Expect ~3 months of consistent training before a real difference — keep going.

The Knack

  • Squeeze just before you cough, sneeze, laugh or lift — it becomes a reflex that stops leaks.

See a pelvic physiotherapist if

  • Exercises hurt, leaking continues past ~12 weeks, or you feel heaviness / bulging in the vagina.
This app is a training timer, not medical advice. Based on NHS / NICE-aligned pelvic floor muscle training guidance (10s holds ×10 + 10 quick squeezes, 3 sessions daily, built up gradually).
